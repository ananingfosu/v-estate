Buy

Buying that new home

Get Started

The Valley Estate will never rush buyers in the process of finding their home. Our proven strategies give our clients the competitive advantage in this market climate. We go the extra mile to identify off-market and overlooked properties and present the best of what satisfy the ideal home.Here is a buyers guide to purchasing a home.

1. Prepration and Consultation

This is a critical stage when looking to buy a home or property. First and foremost, the more important step is to sit down with your agent to discuss goals, timeline, and criteria. Review qualified agencies to really get an understanding of their terms and potential benefits from invest in that firm.

2. Preview Property Listings

Properties move quickly in the real estate market. As such, you should regularly attend to and preview property listings on the market with or without your agent. You can do this across different agencies to compare prices and transactions. Also be sure to note any off-market listings that fit your criteria.

3. Selection Disclosure

In the real estate market, the sellers prepare a Disclosure Package for potential buyers, which contains vital documentation about the condition, history, and restrictions surrounding a property. It is important to review the Disclosure Package thoroughly for any properties under consideration. It is advised.

4. Contract and Sale Process

Most properties will set an offer date. After reviewing the Disclosure Package, your agent will draft your purchase contract to submit with your offer. You will also need to include your pre-approval letter and often times the sales process is very stressful but withe right route and guidance, it is not.

5. Contract and Submission

Once your offer is accepted, your contract is considered "ratified". You will submit a contract deposit in the amount of 3% of the purchase price into escrow, and your closing will take place within a week to a month. Once you have a ratified contract, you will submit it to your lender. The bank will order an appraisal and start the process of underwriting your loan.

6. walk-through and Final Signing

Closing happens in several steps. Sellers sign the closing documents first, a few days before COE. Buyer will then perform a final walk-through of the property and sign their bank and closing statements. The final step is for the Title company to record the new deed, and the keys be given to buyer
